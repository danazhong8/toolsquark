Frequently Asked Questions
When does biological ovulation typically happen?

Biologically, ovulation reliably takes place exactly 14 days prior to the start of your subsequent menstrual cycle phase, independent of overall cycle extensions.

How can acute stress delay a calendar period?

Elevated cortisol levels directly suppress upstream hypothalamic signaling (GnRH), temporarily preventing the hormonal surges necessary to trigger timely ovulation.
