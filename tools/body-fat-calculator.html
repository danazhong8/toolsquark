Frequently Asked Questions

How do I take these measurements to minimize margin of error?

Accuracy is vital for logarithmic calculations. Measure your neck just below the larynx (voice box), keeping the tape level. For males, measure the waist horizontally directly on the navel line; for females, measure at the narrowest point of the natural waistline. Keep the tape completely flat against bare skin, snug enough to rest comfortably but never compressing the tissue. Take measurements in the morning before eating.

Why does the system require gender selection?

Biological fat distribution profiles diverge fundamentally due to human evolutionary physiology. Females naturally require and store additional essential reproductive adipose tissue layers along structural hip and pelvic regions. The U.S. Navy algorithm handles these differences with distinct logarithmic calculation scales to ensure individual precision.

Why is Body Fat Percentage vastly superior to standard BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) considers only your overall mass against height, classifying structural lean muscle mass and dense bone structure identically to adipose storage. This creates a flaw where highly fit, muscular individuals are falsely labeled "overweight." Isolating body fat metrics ensures you track the exact tissues impacting systemic cardiovascular and metabolic health.

Academic Reference Standards: Calculations are programmed in accordance with the U.S. Navy Circumference Formula, originally structured by the Naval Health Research Center. Categorization benchmarks align directly with clinical evaluation guidelines established by the American Council on Exercise (ACE) and the National Institutes of Health (NIH).
