🔍 Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard formula used for Body Mass Index?

Our calculation architecture strictly enforces the standard clinical formula established by the World Health Organization (WHO). For the metric system, the equation is rendered as follows: $$BMI = \frac{weight(kg)}{height(m)^2}$$ For the imperial configuration, a conversion scaler is introduced: $$BMI = \frac{weight(lbs) \times 703}{height(in)^2}$$

What is considered a scientifically healthy BMI score range?

According to biological consensus, scores below 18.5 fall into the Underweight category. Indexes scaling between 18.5 and 24.9 signify an optimal Normal Weight zone. Scores pacing from 25 to 29.9 mark the Overweight range, while anything tracking at 30 or higher enters the Obese threshold.

Can a standard BMI calculation be inaccurate for bodybuilders?

Yes, absolutely. The BMI index cannot distinguish between skeletal muscle tissue and fatty adipose mass. Because muscle tissue is inherently denser than fat tissue, muscular athletes, bodybuilders, and powerlifters often scale into the "Overweight" or "Obese" categories despite maintaining low body fat indices.
