Frequently Asked Questions
What are the standard BMI weight classifications?

According to global health criteria: Underweight is defined as below 18.5, Healthy Weight spans 18.5–24.9, Overweight covers 25–29.9, and Obesity is categorized at 30 or above.

Why can BMI sometimes be inaccurate for athletes?

BMI calculates purely based on total weight and height parameters. Because muscle mass is significantly denser than fat tissue, highly muscular individuals or athletes may be categorized as 'overweight' despite having low body fat levels.
