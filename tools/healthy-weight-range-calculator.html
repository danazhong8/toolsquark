Frequently Asked Questions

How is this medical healthy weight range defined?

The system calculates the exact upper and lower weight boundaries that correspond to a body mass index (BMI) between 18.5 and 24.9, which is universally defined by clinical guidelines as the healthy weight spectrum for adults.

Should my goal be to hit the exact calculated midpoint?

Not necessarily. The midpoint value is a helpful mathematical reference target, but natural variations in dense bone density, fluid levels, and underlying muscle mass can shift your personal ideal weight slightly higher or lower.

Why is mapping weight to height better than standard scale tracking?

Tracking your weight without factoring in height makes it impossible to evaluate your health profile accurately. Linking weight directly to your height helps ensure that your target mass aligns with your physical proportions, helping you manage potential long-term cardiovascular risks effectively.
