Frequently Asked Questions

What is Naegele's Rule?

It is the standard obstetric method estimating delivery by adding 7 days to the first day of your last menstrual period (LMP) and subtracting 3 months.

Will I give birth exactly on this day?

Only about 4% of entries deliver on the exact predicted date. Delivery within two weeks before or after is highly standard and medically expected.

How many weeks long is a standard full-term pregnancy?

A standard clinical full-term pregnancy is calculated as approximately 280 days, which is equivalent to 40 weeks from the first day of your last menstrual period.
