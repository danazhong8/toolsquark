    How To Read This Result

    This original 5-item educational self-check reflects your answers for the past 2 weeks. It reviews Fear of Judgment, Avoidance, Post-Event Replay.

    The overall score runs from 5 to 20. Result bands are descriptive groupings for self-reflection, not clinical cutoffs. Dimension labels are calculated from the questions assigned to each area.

    Important limit: This is not a validated screening instrument and cannot diagnose, rule out, or measure the severity of a medical or mental health condition. Use the result as a prompt for reflection, not as a label.

    What This Self-Check Covers

    The five questions review fear of judgment, avoidance, self-monitoring, difficulty speaking in groups, and replaying interactions afterward.

    This is an original ToolsQuark checklist. It is not SPIN, LSAS, MINI, or a diagnostic interview for social anxiety disorder.

    How Scoring Works

    Each answer contributes 1 to 4 points, for a total from 5 to 20. Higher totals reflect more frequent social-fear responses in this question set.

    • 5-8: fewer frequent social-fear signals.
    • 9-14: several recurring signals.
    • 15-20: many frequent signals that may be limiting.
    The bands and dimension bars are editorial, not validated diagnostic thresholds.

    Shyness, Stress, And Social Anxiety

    Temporary nervousness, introversion, unfamiliar settings, communication differences, trauma, depression, and autism-related experiences can overlap with these answers. Clinical assessment considers persistence, distress, avoidance, impairment, and alternative explanations.

    When To Seek Support

    Qualified support may help when social fear repeatedly blocks school, work, healthcare, relationships, or ordinary activities. A clinician can discuss evidence-based options without treating this score as a diagnosis.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is shyness the same as social anxiety?

    No. Social anxiety involves fear, distress, avoidance, or impairment around social evaluation.

    Can this diagnose social anxiety disorder?

    No. It is a private educational self-check, not a clinical diagnosis.
