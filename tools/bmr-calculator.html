Frequently Asked Questions

What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?

[cite: 2]Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the minimum number of calories your body requires to perform vital life-sustaining functions, such as breathing, circulating blood, and regulating body temperature, while at complete rest.

How can I use BMR to lose weight?

To lose weight, you must first calculate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) by multiplying your BMR by an activity factor. Once you have your TDEE, maintain a caloric deficit by consuming fewer calories than your TDEE baseline.

What is the difference between BMR and RMR?

While both estimate calories burned at rest, BMR is measured under strict, restrictive laboratory conditions after 12 hours of fasting. Resting Metabolic Rate (RMR) is measured under looser conditions and usually tracks slightly higher than BMR.

Can I increase my Basal Metabolic Rate?

Yes. The most effective way to boost your BMR permanently is to increase lean muscle mass through strength training. Muscle tissue is more metabolically active than fat tissue, meaning it burns more calories even when you are asleep.
