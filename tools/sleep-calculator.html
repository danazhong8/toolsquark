Frequently Asked Questions

What is a sleep cycle?

A complete sleep cycle is often described as lasting roughly 90 minutes and includes light sleep, deeper sleep, and REM stages.

Why can I feel tired after 8 hours?

You may wake during a deeper stage instead of near the end of a cycle. Cycle timing can help, though sleep quality and consistency matter too.

Does everyone have exactly 90-minute cycles?

No. Cycle length varies by person, age, sleep pressure, and night timing. This calculator uses 90 minutes as a simple planning estimate.
