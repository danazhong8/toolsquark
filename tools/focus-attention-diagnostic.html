Frequently Asked Questions

What is executive dysfunction in attention planning?

Executive dysfunction refers to a structural failure in the brain's ability to initiate tasks, allocate working memory efficiently, organize long-term workflows, and filter out environmental noise.[cite: 13]

How does multi-tasking impact deep cognitive work?

True multitasking is a neurological impossibility for deep cognition. Instead, the brain rapidly switches tasks, leaving an attentive residue that compromises performance quality.[cite: 13]

What environment baseline boosts task initiation speed?

Clear working memory overhead by utilizing micro-lists, placing digital triggers entirely out of sight, and establishing fixed, quiet time blocks to lower task friction.[cite: 13]
