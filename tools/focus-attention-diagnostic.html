    How To Read This Result

    This original 5-item educational self-check reflects your answers for the past 2 weeks. It reviews Distractibility, Task Initiation Friction, Task Switching.

    The overall score runs from 5 to 20. Result bands are descriptive groupings for self-reflection, not clinical cutoffs. Dimension labels are calculated from the questions assigned to each area.

    Important limit: This is not a validated screening instrument and cannot diagnose, rule out, or measure the severity of a medical or mental health condition. Use the result as a prompt for reflection, not as a label.

    What This Self-Check Covers

    The five questions review distractibility, task initiation, task switching, sustained focus, and priority clarity during the stated timeframe.

    This is an original ToolsQuark checklist. It is not ASRS, a neuropsychological test, or an ADHD diagnostic interview.

    How Scoring Works

    Answers are scored from 1 to 4, producing a total from 5 to 20. Higher totals reflect more frequent attention friction in this question set.

    • 5-8: fewer frequent focus difficulties.
    • 9-14: several recurring attention barriers.
    • 15-20: many frequent barriers that may warrant support.
    The score bands and dimension bars are editorial, not validated ADHD thresholds.

    Attention Problems Have Many Causes

    Sleep loss, anxiety, depression, stress, pain, substances, medication effects, learning differences, and an overloaded environment can all affect attention. ADHD diagnosis also considers childhood history, persistence across settings, functional impairment, and alternative explanations.

    When To Seek Evaluation

    Consider qualified evaluation when attention problems are long-standing, occur across settings, or substantially affect school, work, finances, relationships, driving, or safety. Bring concrete examples and a timeline rather than relying on this score alone.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Does low focus mean ADHD?

    Not necessarily. Sleep, stress, environment, mood, workload, and habits can all affect focus.

    What is the fastest focus improvement?

    Remove one major distraction and define the first tiny action before using a timer.
