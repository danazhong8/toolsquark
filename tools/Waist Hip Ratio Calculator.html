🔍 Frequently Asked Questions

What is the scientific significance of the Waist-to-Hip Ratio (WHR)?

The Waist-to-Hip Ratio (WHR) is a clinical epidemiological metric utilized to evaluate body fat distribution. Unlike the Body Mass Index (BMI), which measures general adipose mass based solely on height and weight, WHR isolates abdominal adiposity. Higher scores point significantly toward abdominal visceral fat storage surrounding essential internal organs, serving as a powerful, independent diagnostic predictor for cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, and type 2 diabetes.

What are the official World Health Organization (WHO) health risk cut-off thresholds for WHR?

According to historical research guidelines released by the World Health Organization (WHO), a waist-to-hip ratio equal to or greater than 0.90 for male adults, and equal to or greater than 0.85 for female adults, indicates central abdominal obesity and substantially elevated metabolic health complications. Ratios sitting firmly under these respective gender benchmarks reflect a lower biological risk configuration.

How do I accurately measure my waist and hip circumferences for this calculator?

To acquire precise physical diagnostic tracking results, stand straight and wrap a non-elastic tape measure directly around your bare skin. For your waist circumference, position the tape mid-way between your lowest palpable rib and the top edge of your hip bone (iliac crest), measuring at the end of a normal exhalation. For your hip circumference, wrap the tape snugly around the widest horizontal perimeter of your buttocks. Ensure the measuring loop stays parallel to the floor during both steps.
