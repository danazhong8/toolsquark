🔍 Frequently Asked Questions

What is the scientific formula behind this Macro Calculator?

Our calculation engine utilizes the highly accurate Mifflin-St Jeor equation to establish your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), factoring in biological gender, age, weight, and height. It then applies a standardized activity multiplier to find your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). Based on your selected goal, we adjust the caloric intake and split the macronutrients into a balanced, clinically supported moderate ratio: approximately 30% Protein, 35% Fats, and 35% Carbohydrates, ensuring optimal metabolic function and muscle retention.

Why are macronutrients measured in grams when calculating calories?

Macronutrients are the primary chemical compounds the human body consumes in large quantities to produce cellular energy. They possess distinct thermodynamic values. Both Protein and Carbohydrates yield approximately 4 kilocalories per gram, while Dietary Fats are more energy-dense, providing roughly 9 kilocalories per gram. By reverse-engineering your caloric target using these constants, our calculator provides actionable gram-based targets for meal prep and food tracking.

How frequently should I adjust my daily macro targets?

As your body undergoes physical composition changes—such as shedding adipose tissue or synthesizing new lean muscle mass—your Basal Metabolic Rate dynamically shifts. This phenomenon is known as metabolic adaptation. Clinical dietitians generally recommend recalculating your TDEE and macro distribution every 4 to 6 weeks, or after every 5 pounds (approx. 2.2 kg) of weight fluctuation, to prevent dreaded performance plateaus.
