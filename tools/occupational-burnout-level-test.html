    How Scoring Works

    Answers are scored from 1 to 4, producing a total from 5 to 20. Higher totals represent more frequent burnout-related responses in this checklist.

    • 5-8: fewer frequent signals.
    • 9-14: several warning signals.
    • 15-20: many frequent signals needing attention.
    These bands and dimension bars are editorial, not validated occupational cutoffs.

    Burnout Is An Occupational Phenomenon

    The WHO describes burn-out in ICD-11 as an occupational phenomenon resulting from chronic workplace stress that has not been successfully managed; it is not classified as a medical condition. Similar symptoms can also occur with depression, anxiety, sleep disorders, illness, caregiving strain, or unsafe work.

    What To Do With The Result

    Look for changes to workload, control, staffing, role clarity, recovery time, fairness, and support rather than placing the entire burden on personal resilience. Seek professional help when symptoms affect health or functioning, and use workplace or emergency channels for immediate safety risks.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is burnout just being tired?

    No. Burnout often combines exhaustion, cynicism or detachment, and reduced professional efficacy.

    Can a vacation fix burnout?

    Rest can help, but persistent burnout often requires workload, boundary, role, or support changes.
