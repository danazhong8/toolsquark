Frequently Asked Questions

When does peak ovulation typically occur?

Biological ovulation reliably takes place approximately 14 days before your next menstrual cycle phase begins, though this can vary slightly based on individual cycle consistency[cite: 14]. Fluctuations in rest cycles or high stress can alter this timeline.

How can acute lifestyle stress delay a calendar period?

High cortisol surges triggered by psychological or physical stress can directly suppress upstream hypothalamic signaling, which temporarily disrupts and delays regular hormonal ovulation cycles[cite: 14].

What is considered a normal menstrual cycle length?

A standard medical menstrual cycle typically ranges between 21 to 35 days for healthy adults, with bleeding lasting anywhere from 2 to 7 days[cite: 14]. Variances outside this spectrum should be observed over a consecutive 90-day tracking log.
