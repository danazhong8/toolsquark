🔍 Frequently Asked Questions

What scientific formula is used to calculate my daily calories?

Our calculator strictly adheres to the highly accurate Mifflin-St Jeor equation to establish your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), before applying a metabolic activity multiplier. For adult males, the clinical mathematical representation is: $$BMR = (10 \times weight\ in\ kg) + (6.25 \times height\ in\ cm) - (5 \times age\ in\ years) + 5$$ For females, the baseline formula adjusts to account for distinct structural physiology: $$BMR = (10 \times weight\ in\ kg) + (6.25 \times height\ in\ cm) - (5 \times age\ in\ years) - 161$$

How many calories do I need to consume to lose weight?

Weight management is fundamentally governed by the law of thermodynamics and caloric deficit. Once this calculator outputs your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) maintenance calories, a safe and sustainable biological strategy is to subtract approximately 300 to 500 calories from that target. Dropping your intake drastically below your core BMR is strongly discouraged, as it can trigger severe muscle catabolism and metabolic adaptation.

Why do men and women have different caloric requirements?

The difference in metabolic demand fundamentally traces back to natural body composition. Skeletal muscle tissue is highly metabolically active and requires significantly more continuous energy to sustain than adipose (fat) tissue. Men, biologically, tend to possess higher lean muscle mass and structural bone density compared to women of the exact same height and weight, necessitating a naturally elevated basal calorie burn rate.
