Frequently Asked Questions

How does this macro calculator work?

It first estimates daily energy needs with the Mifflin-St Jeor equation and activity multipliers. It then adjusts calories based on your goal and applies a moderate macro split of 30% protein, 35% fat, and 35% carbohydrates.

Are these macros exact prescriptions?

No. They are practical planning estimates. Real needs vary with training, digestion, food preferences, adherence, and medical context.

Should I choose fat loss, maintenance, or muscle gain?

Choose fat loss for a planned calorie deficit, maintenance when you want weight stability, and muscle gain when you are pairing a calorie surplus with progressive resistance training.

Methodology & Quality Assurance: This tool combines Mifflin-St Jeor calorie estimation with standard energy values: protein and carbohydrates provide about 4 kcal per gram, while fat provides about 9 kcal per gram.

Medical Disclaimer: These results are educational estimates and not medical nutrition therapy. Consult a qualified clinician or registered dietitian for personal guidance.
