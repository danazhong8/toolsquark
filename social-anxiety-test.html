Behavioral Insights

Social anxiety is rooted in the fear of exclusion; your brain misinterprets minor social awkwardness as a threat to survival.
Safety behaviors (like staring at your phone or staying near walls) offer short-term comfort but reinforce social fears long-term.
People pay far less attention to your flaws than you think; this cognitive distortion is known as the spotlight effect.

FAQ

Is introversion the same as social anxiety?
No. Introverts prefer quiet settings because they recharge alone, but they don't necessarily fear social judgment. Social anxiety is driven by dread.
Can social anxiety be outgrown naturally?
Mild forms settle with age, but stubborn anxiety requires gradual exposure to social triggers to rewire your brain's fear response.
What should I do when experiencing a social panic spike?
Shift your focus outward. Describe 3 physical objects in the room to break your internal anxious monitoring loop.
