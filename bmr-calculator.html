Understanding BMR vs. TDEE

  • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): The basic amount of energy your physical architecture expends at complete rest to execute involuntary physiological life-support operations (breathing, core temperature regulation, cellular regeneration).
  • TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure): The summation of your baseline BMR parameters multiplied by your daily physical activities scale. This serves as your calorie maintenance target.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which scientific formula is utilized?

This application implements the recognized Mifflin-St Jeor equation matrix, proven to supply highly reliable metrics across international nutritional clinical frameworks.

How to modify weight parameters using this data?

To reduce overall mass, introduce a safe caloric deficit below your evaluated TDEE limits (typically 300 to 500 kcal). To gain mass, construct a clean surplus structure.
