FAQ

What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?
BMR represents the exact number of calories your body requires to perform its most basic life-sustaining functions—such as breathing, circulating blood, and cell production—while at complete rest, without any digestive activity.
How do I use this score to lose or gain weight?
Your BMR is the foundation. First, calculate your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) based on your activity level. To safely lose fat, create a modest deficit of 300-500 calories below your TDEE, but never eat less than your bare BMR score.
Why does age affect my BMR score?
As the body matures, lean muscle mass naturally tends to decrease while adipose tissue increases. Since muscle is highly metabolically active compared to fat, BMR naturally experiences a gradual decline over decades.
